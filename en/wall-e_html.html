The FUTURE of our PLANET

Can we know what TOMORROW WILL BE LIKE

Our planet could not stay the same. Since the 19th century, the effects of global warming have increased considerably until today. This acceleration is responsible for the climatic imbalance, the consequences of which could modify the landscape in a brutal way, making the conditions of life on earth increasingly complex.
What could our earth look like in the next few years?

A dried earth

Drought is not related to the absence of rain, but to the increase in heat. From the 1970s, the temperature increased by +1°. A figure that seems insignificant, but which warms our summers. A warmer climate means an increase in water evaporation. From the ocean but also from the ground. With the hottest summers, the terrestrial drought weakens the vegetation, limits our harvests and increases the risk of forest fires.

A land of WASTE

Our waste is the second major problem of our world, and their management is just as important. 2,000,000,000 tons of waste are produced on average per year and could increase by +70% in 2050. Plastic waste represents only 12% of our production, and yet it is the major problem of our management. Only 20% of them are recycled. The rest is either buried (37%), stored (33%) or burned (11%). Their productions are increasingly important, with a very long decomposition time, which could well lead to pyramids of waste.
