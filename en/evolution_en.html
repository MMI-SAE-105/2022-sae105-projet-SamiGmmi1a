EVOLUTION of the HUMAN SPECIES.

The GENEALOGY OF THE HUMAN SPECIES.

7 billions years is the time that separates us between our era and the beginning of the human species. Throughout this period, about twenty hominids followed one another, divided into three main groups: 1. The first Hominids 2. The Australopithecines 3 The Homos.

The STEPS of EVOLUTION

The first human remains dating back 7 billions years have been found in Africa.
In particular skulls, similar to monkeys, and recognizable by their smaller cranial box.
Then came the Australopithecines, having lived between 4.4 and 4.1 billion years ago. Again, features similar to monkeys. But an innovation in this species. This is the advent of bipedalism.
The last phase is that of the genus Homo. Who lived on Earth 2 billion years ago. It is the first species to adopt hunting tools, but also to master fire. Thanks to its adaptation to its environment, this species will spread throughout the world to evolve from Homo-sapiens to our current species.
