Our Neighbor Planets

Are we ALONE IN THE UNIVERS?

Our solar system is not the only one in the universe. And this theory isn't new. Scientists had already hypothesized of planets similar to our one, elsewhere in the universe, long before science-Fiction sagas. The first discovery of an exoplanet dates from 1995, baptized: 51 PEGASIS B.
Our universe has no less than a trillion of exoplanet, dispersed to the four corners of cosmos, represent an infinity of galaxy.

CONDITIONS for a LIVEABLE PLANET

To be habitable, a planet must meet several criteria:
  • - It must be rocky
  • - Have liquid water to facilitate encounters between molecules.
  • - Have a mass close to that of the earth. Too massive, it attracts gas around it, turning it into a gaseous planet.
  • - The distance between it and the sun. Too close, the temperature could be raised to over 426°C. But near the sun, the temperature could drop below 140°
  • - Finally, it must revolve around a medium-sized star. Large stars are not an option, dwarf stars also do not fit due to strong X-ray emission
