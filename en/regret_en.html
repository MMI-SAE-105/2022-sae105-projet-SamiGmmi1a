Are we GONNA KILL our PLANET?

"There is no planet B". This quote is not unknown to you. This means one thing; no planet is as indispensable as Earth. But the consequences of pollution are almost real and could cause the disappearance of Man, or even of any type of life present. We know the risks, but will we be able to avoid them? Over time, climate anomalies have become increasingly violent. X'plore summarizes for you, in this article, the reasons for such an increase in climate problems.
Since the 1970s, increasing heat has altered the water and precipitation cycle. As the air warms, it absorbs water vapour, which condenses to form rain clouds. The warmer the air, the more water vapor there is in the atmosphere. The clouds must then evacuate the water contained by the rains. The greater the evaporation, the more violent the rains, multiplying the number of precipitations and floods.
Conversely, hot areas will continue to dry out. Due to the high heat, the water evaporates faster. The problem is the rainy seasons, which are no longer constant. This natural disturbance will give way to arid landscapes in some parts of the globe.
With the increase in heat, the snow melts faster in summer. Water flowing from the mountains is redirected to the oceans, where it will evaporate. During the winter rainy season, the rain turns to snow to settle on top of the mountains. So far, nothing out of the ordinary. The only problem is the duration of hot periods and rainy seasons. The heat is longer than the winter period, and the snow does not have time to form.
