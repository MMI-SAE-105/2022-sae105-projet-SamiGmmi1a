ISS: a GREAT ADVENTURE

It is POSSIBLE to TRAVEL in SPACE?

November the 20th 1998, this is the put into orbit of the International Space Station. A common project between three big nations: the United States of America, Russia, Canada, and European Union.
Today, it represents 6 different countries who collaborate in the elaboration of ISS. Since, this laboratory haven't stop to getting bigger measuring around 109-meters length, 73-meters width et 33-meters high. A total whose weight exceeds 400 tons, representing the most expensive space station never built: 150 billion of dollars.

Prepare for the FUTUR of SPACE EXPLORATION

Laboratory? Yes, because it is above all its first function. For more than 10 years, 232 astronauts of 18 different nationalities have stayed there to conduct experiments against the negative effect of space on humans.
Fields are varied but the target goal is to understand the body reaction in space conditions. Many discovers became useful on earth. Some of thesis's experiences could be a cure again brain tumour.
But the base is approaching to its deterioration phase. All projects aboard ISS could stop for 2025. The base will be sent to the sun for disappear. But just before it happened, many countries keep hights hopes for scientific advances.
The goal? Being able to stay more than 6 months in space
6 months. This is what it takes for travel to mars from the earth. But such a trip represents an immense challenge for our body in space environments…

The HISTOIRY oh the SPACE STATION

X

1971: Salyout

Salyut is the first space station send in space. After a lot of failures of the Soviet Union, Salyut take of in April 1971, the 19th with a Proton-K rocket. But the mission could not go to the end. The hatch did not open when the crew wanted to get inside the station, forcing them back to earth.

Salyout
X

1973: Skylab

The year following the Salyut mission, the Americans will also send their space station. The project is to study the living conditions in space and to carry out experiments there to send a human to the moon. The project will last until July 11, 1979.

Skylab
X

1975: Apollo-Salyout

Apollo-Soyuz is the first unifying space project between the USA and the USSR. The crew is made up of two Russians, Alexei Leonov and Valeri Koubassov, and three Americans, Thomas Stafford, Vance Brand and Donald Slayton. Two launches took place: the first in Baikonur, on July 15, 1975, and the second from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida, on July 17, 1975. Once in space, Soyuz and Apollo will join to form only a station.

Apollo-Salyout
X

1986: Mir

Launch in orbit February 1986, the 19th by the Soviet Union and achieved in 1996, Mir is the first space project directly assembled from the space. This station has allowed to Russians to lead their own experiences in biology, physical, astronomy and meteorology. At the end of the Soviet Block, the Mir Station will welcome in it centre different crews with different origins: North American, European, Japanese, Indian and Slovak. This project will end in mars 2001, the 23rd.

La station Mir
X

1988: Freedome

It is the first permanent space station in Earth orbit. A project announced by President Ronald Reagan in 1984. The European Space Agency joined the project on January 31, 1985, followed by Canada and Japan the same year. The first module will be sent into orbit on July 16, 1986. It was when Russia joined the project that it was renamed the International Space Station.

Freedome
X

1998: Zarya

The first module of the International Space Station, sent by the Russian Company GNPKZ Krounichev, on November 20, 1998. The purpose of this mission is to reduce the manufacturing cost by assembling the Space Station as you go. Therefore, Zarya will be joined by Unity, the following year, in 1984.

Zarya
X

1998: Unity

Two weeks after orbiting Zarya, the United States launched the Unity module in December 1998. One module serving as a staging point for the next module. The set is composed of 6 mooring bridge where the modules will be assembled from outside the station.

Unity
X

2000: Zvesda

Built by the Russian company RKK Enigma, the Zvesda took off in July 2000, the 12th, and moors two weeks later. Zvesda is the third module assembled to the station. It was conceived to replace the first Zarya module but will be finely used as a main laboratory.

Zvezda
X

2005: STS-114

The crew counted 7 seven members, including two women and a Japanese: Eileen Collins, James Kelly, Soichi Noguchi, Stephen Robinson, Andrew Thomas, Wendy Laurence et Charles Camarda. The goal of the mission was to bring back the necessary supplies on board the station. The crew will also benefit to repair the outside modules during an outing of 6 hours and 30 minutes.

STS-114
X

2016: BEAM

The BEAM project (Bigelow Expendable Activity Module) started in April 2016. This mission was experimental only. During the two years of the test, nothing has been stocked in the inside of the module. The crew members have penetrated inside only for measuring the temperature, the pression, and the radiation dangers inside the structure.

BEAM
X

2020: CRS-21

Sent into space on December 5, 2020, by the private company SpaceX, the Falcon 9 rocket carried the Bishop module on board. The latter will be docked with the ISS on December 19, 2020. It will allow the transfer of cubic-shaped nanosatellites to carry out measurements and scientific studies on space environmental conditions, at low cost.

CRS-21

2021: Nauka

This recent module serves as a work room, and a rest room, for the current crew. A project carried out by the Russian Space Agency, the date of which was scheduled for 2007. The same year, the Russian Space Agency is in negotiation with the ISS, which will postpone the project to 2009, then 2014. Due to a failure of the propulsion system, the project will be postponed for 2017, and 2018. This same year, by the duration of the work, the launch will be scheduled for 2020. Russia confirms a new launch date for July 29, 2021.
