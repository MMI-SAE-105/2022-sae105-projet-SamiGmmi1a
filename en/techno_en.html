3 INCREDIBLE INVENTIONS of ISS

The NEW DISCOVERIES of TOMORROW

Weightlessness makes possible what is impossible on earth. With its weightless framework and its suitable environment, the ISS base is the ideal place where experiments, impossible on earth, can be carried out in space. Here are 3 incredible discoveries in space.

The INVENTION that will MAKE our TOMORROW

3. Telemachus:
This is the invention that we will remember the most. Being able to manipulate objects without touching them, only with acoustic waves. It is a French innovation tested by Tomas PESQUET. On land, the invention did not enjoy blatant success. The different forces exerted by the Earth's attraction complicate the process. But in the ISS, this technology could be developed there, the first tests turned out to be successful.
2. the ISS vegetable garden:
On board the ISS, the first space lettuces were tasted. A feat. To colonize other planets, we must be able to produce our own food resources. Transporting all the necessary supplies would require too much energy, future astronauts will have to live in total autonomy. However, the conditions of a culture without gravity are still unknown to us. This experience gives a first idea of ​​a culture in orbit and to know the conditions necessary for its agriculture. The experiment was carried out on July 7, 2015, for 33 days. The results are positive, the scientists are hopeful and hope to be able to grow fruit on board the station.
1. Dark matter:
After an 18-month study, the astronauts aboard the ISS explain that they have observed the existence of dark matter. By definition, what is opposed to the “ordinary” matter that surrounds us and that we can see (atoms, stars, planets…). This discovery is by far the most important of all. According to the Big Bang theory, after the creation of the universe, different molecules would escape and clump together with dark matter. The energy fluctuations produced during this fusion would be responsible for our existence.
