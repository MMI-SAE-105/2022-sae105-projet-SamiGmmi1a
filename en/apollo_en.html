APOLLO 11 MISSION

The greatest project of the HISTORY.

The space travel initiative was a goal launched by President John F. Kennedy in 1961. 8 years later, on July 16, 1969, the Saturn 5 space rocket had just lifted off the ground of Earth. 4 days later, on July 20, 1969 at 5:17 p.m., astronauts Neil A. Armstrong and Edwin E. Aldrin were the first men to set foot on the lunar surface.
The mission is a success. Since then, the idea of building a city on the moon has become a goal for the space industry.
Samples collected during the Apollo 11 mission have shed light on the origins of the moon, which appeared 4.5 trillion years ago when a meteorite collided with "earth".

To Who will BELONG the MOON?

Space belongs to no one. In 1966, the first space treaties were signed, stipulating that space resources were not the property of anyone. However, one could believe in a race towards the moon against all the nations.
Number 1, it is the United States which, with the research of NASA and private companies such as SpaceX by Elon Musk or Blue Origin by Jeff Bezos, who have placed their technologies at the forefront. In second place, China, which invests great efforts in the success of more and more space missions. In third position, thre Europe, which aims for the moon by 2030, counting on the support of all the major European countries.
We could also mention Japan, Russia, India and the Arab Emirates, which are constantly pushing their limits to set another foot on the moon.
