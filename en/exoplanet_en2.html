What do we know about EXOPLNETS?

EXOPLANETS SECRETS

Exoplanets are no longer unknown to us. Yet they still remain a mystery to us. The distance that separates us is counted in light years. Several centuries would be needed for a probe to reach the nearest exoplanet: Proxima Centauri B. Fortunately, many methods exist to discover these mysterious stars.

3 ways to DISCOVER NEW PLANETS

3. Transit:
The principle is simple. It is the detection of a shadow from a planet when it passes between it sun and our planet. Its shadow causes a little black stain moving in front of the star around it gravitate.
2. Radial velocity:
The sun is not fixe in the middle of the solar system. The planets gravitated around cause it oscillation. By observing the movements of a star, we can deduce the presence of a planet around. The effect gave the impression the sun is always trying to keep the distance between it and other planets.
1. Spectroscopy:
Although the name sounds complex, the system is simple. It is necessary to decompose the light of a star with a prism. Just before reaching our planet, this light passes through the atmosphere of another surrounding planet. The principle is to observe the absorption rays, which create small black lines by absorbing colours. Each molecule absorbs its own colour. Depending on the missing colours, we can define the composition of the atmosphere form a planet.
